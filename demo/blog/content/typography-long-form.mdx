Good reading typography is invisible. The reader simply keeps going.

## A starting scale

- Body text at 1.125rem with a 1.7 line-height.
- A measure of 60–75 characters per line.
- Headings on a distinct display face for rhythm.

Contrast and spacing carry far more weight than any decorative flourish.

## Beyond Latin: tagging foreign scripts

The body font is self-hosted with the `latin` and `latin-ext` subsets, so
romanized transliteration renders in-font — for example *Atha Yogānuśāsanam*
(ā ī ū ṛ ṅ ś ṣ ḥ). For non-Latin scripts, this site declares a `scriptFonts`
entry (Noto Serif Devanagari) and tags individual spans with a `lang` attribute
and the generated `.font-devanagari` class:

> <span lang="sa" class="font-devanagari">अथ योगानुशासनम् ॥१॥</span>
>
> **1. Atha Yogānuśāsanam.** — *now, the exposition of Yoga.*

Only the page that needs it ships the extra font, and only the tagged span
switches families. Everything else keeps the body face.
