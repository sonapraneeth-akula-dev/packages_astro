The satteri Markdown processor wires `remark-math` and `rehype-katex` into the
pipeline, so any `.md` or `.mdx` post can contain LaTeX. The math is rendered to
HTML **at build time** by KaTeX — there is no runtime library to download and no
flash of unstyled formulae.

## Inline math

Wrap an expression in single dollar signs to keep it in the flow of a sentence.
The mass–energy equivalence $E = mc^2$ sits on the baseline, and so does the
golden ratio $\varphi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}$ or a quick sum like
$\sum_{i=1}^{n} i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$.

Inline math inherits the surrounding font size, so it never disturbs the line
height of a paragraph — for example $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ reads naturally mid-clause.

## Block math

Use double dollar signs for display equations. They render centered on their own
line with the standard KaTeX display spacing.

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \, dx = \sqrt{\pi}
$$

Multi-line derivations work too, via an `aligned` environment:

$$
\begin{aligned}
  (a + b)^2 &= a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \\
  (a - b)^2 &= a^2 - 2ab + b^2 \\
  a^2 - b^2 &= (a + b)(a - b)
\end{aligned}
$$

Matrices, fractions and Greek all render as expected:

$$
A = \begin{bmatrix}
  \alpha & \beta \\
  \gamma & \delta
\end{bmatrix}
\qquad
\det(A) = \alpha\delta - \beta\gamma
$$

## Escaping dollar signs

To show a literal dollar sign instead of opening a math span, escape it with a
backslash: a coffee costs \$3.50. Only unescaped `$`/`$$` pairs are treated as
math delimiters.
